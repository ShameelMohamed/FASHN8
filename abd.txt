🔥 Stronger, Smarter Dashboard Ideas
1️⃣ Wardrobe Utilization Score (Core Metric)

This is clean and powerful.

Metric:
Utilization % = 
(Number of unique items worn this month) 
/ 
(Total items in wardrobe)

Example:

20 items owned

12 worn this month
→ 60% utilization

This directly shows:

Underused clothes

Closet inefficiency

Optimization opportunity

This is responsible AI thinking.

2️⃣ Outfit Diversity Index

Measure:

How often the same pair is repeated

How many unique combinations generated

Metric:

Unique outfit combinations / Total outfit days

Now AI is helping maximize wardrobe combinatorics.

That’s mathematically clean.

3️⃣ Cost-Per-Wear Estimator (User-Entered Prices)

Optional:
Let users add approximate purchase price.

Then compute:

Cost per wear = price / times worn

This is powerful and grounded.

It promotes mindful consumption without fake carbon math.

4️⃣ “Dormant Item Alert”

AI identifies:

Items not worn in 30/60 days

Then:

“You haven’t styled this navy shirt in 45 days. Want 3 new combos?”

Now AI actively prevents waste.

That’s real optimization.

5️⃣ Smart Reuse Suggestions

Instead of CO₂ saved, show:

“You styled 3 items in 4 different ways this week.”

“Your wardrobe diversity increased by 18%.”

These are system-grounded metrics.

6️⃣ Before Shopping Warning (Very Strong)

When using Snap Shop:

If similar wardrobe item exists:

“You already own 2 visually similar items.”

This is responsible AI in action.

No fake sustainability stats needed.

7️⃣ Seasonal Rotation Intelligence

AI detects:

Items unused because of season

Suggest:

“These 4 items may be better rotated out temporarily.”

This becomes wardrobe lifecycle management.

💎 If You Still Want Environmental Framing (But Done Properly)

Don’t estimate CO₂ per outfit.

Instead say:

“Extending garment life by 9 months can significantly reduce its environmental impact (industry research). FashN8 focuses on maximizing garment lifespan through intelligent recombination.”

No fake numbers.
Just behavior optimization.

That’s mature and credible.
